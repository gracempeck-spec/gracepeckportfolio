About Me

Grace Peck

Museum Professional  ·  Curator  ·  Archivist  ·  Artist

📍 North Adams, MA  ·  Berkshires

My work has always centered around art, museums, and community, but I have intentionally explored the field from multiple perspectives. I began my studies in Florence, Italy, where I immersed myself in art history, fine arts, and cultural studies. After completing my BFA in Art History, I returned to Italy to earn a Master's degree in Museum Studies, focusing on curation and exhibition development. My dissertation explored exhibition practices and curatorial methodology.

Throughout my career, I have worked across education, archives, curation, and public programming. I began in museum education, working in public programs at The Strong National Museum of Play before continuing as a museum educator at Tang Teaching Museum at Skidmore College and MASS MoCA. These experiences strengthened my passion for making art accessible, engaging, and community-driven.

An opportunity to shift into collections management and preservation led me to become County Archivist for Washington County, where I spent two years rebuilding the archival system, preserving historical documents, and improving public accessibility to the collection. During this time, I also began serving as Curator for the annual Landscapes for Landsakes exhibition hosted by the Agricultural Stewardship Association.

In this role, I oversee every stage of the exhibition process — from artist outreach and exhibition design to installation and public events. The exhibition raises funds for land conservation, bringing together artists, local farms, and the surrounding community in support of the region's landscape and culture.

Now based in North Adams with my partner, I continue my curatorial work while expanding my focus on my own artistic practice. My work has been exhibited in several shows throughout New York, and I am excited to continue building connections within the creative community of the Berkshires.

🌿 What I Care About

My work is grounded in a commitment to art, history, and community — three forces that continually shape and inform one another. Through my experience as a museum educator, I came to understand the importance of making art accessible and engaging for diverse audiences.

In archival work, I saw how access to history and historical records can deepen our understanding of the present and create meaningful personal connections to the past.

Through my curatorial work with the Agricultural Stewardship Association, I have witnessed the unique ability of art to build community, inspire dialogue, and unite people around shared values and collective action.

Art is one of humanity's most enduring forms of connection and expression. My goal is not only to contribute to that creative legacy through my own work, but also to create spaces where others can engage with it, learn from it, and feel connected through it.
