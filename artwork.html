How I Started

Creativity has been a constant presence throughout my life. As a child, I built fairy houses in my parents' yard, spent hours paging through my great-grandfather's 1940s "how to draw" books, and approached every school project as an opportunity to create something imaginative and personal. Those early experiences nurtured both my curiosity and my eye for storytelling through visual detail.

Although I earned a degree in Fine Arts, my academic focus was primarily in Art History. While I explored studio practice through coursework, I did not initially envision myself pursuing art-making professionally. That changed unexpectedly when I was invited to participate in a local exhibition. The experience encouraged me to reconnect with the hands-on creative process and reconsider what it meant to make art as an adult.

I began by drawing portraits inspired by people and cultures from around the world, combining my love of visual storytelling with my interest in history and human connection. Over time, my work expanded to include animals, intimate domestic scenes, and natural spaces. Pet and family portrait commissions allowed me to create deeply personal pieces for others, while my drawings of greenhouses and cozy interiors reflected a longing for warmth, stillness, and connection to nature during the colder months.

My current body of work explores symbolism and storytelling through a reimagined tarot-inspired format. I am developing an original oracle deck that pairs imagery with written meanings, blending illustration, narrative, and personal interpretation.

Nature, nostalgia, and everyday beauty remain at the center of my artistic practice. I am continually inspired by the quiet details of the world around me and strive to create work that feels both whimsical and emotionally grounded.

Open to Commissions

My formal curatorial training began during graduate school while pursuing my Master's degree in Museum Studies. The program emphasized a comprehensive understanding of museum practice, requiring students to engage with every aspect of exhibition development and management. As part of this training, my cohort developed and produced a full exhibition using materials sourced from the archives of an Italian museum. The project involved researching and developing a curatorial concept, designing and installing the exhibition, creating promotional materials, leading tours, and engaging directly with the public. The exhibition even received local media attention in Italy, giving us the opportunity to present our work on Italian television.

This experience inspired me to further explore curatorial theory and practice through my graduate dissertation. In my paper, I developed a complete exhibition proposal and outlined the framework required to execute the project from concept to completion, including research methodology, exhibition design, audience engagement, and logistical planning.

After completing my studies, I continued working within museums through educational and public-facing roles before being approached by the Agricultural Stewardship Association to curate their annual Landscapes for Landsakes exhibition. My responsibilities included artist outreach, coordinating the jury process, receiving and handling artwork, designing an original exhibition layout within a more than 150-year-old barn, and fully installing the show.

Over the past several years, curating this exhibition has become one of the most rewarding aspects of my career. The exhibition not only celebrates the remarkable talent of regional artists, but also raises funds to support land conservation efforts. The most fulfilling part of the experience is witnessing the public's response — seeing visitors engage with the work, connect with the mission behind the exhibition, and experience a sense of wonder at the transformation of the historic barn into a vibrant gallery space.

In addition to my curatorial work with ASA, I have consulted on and contributed to several arts initiatives throughout the region. I curated the Second Annual GALA Art Exhibition at the Greenwich Free Library and have collaborated with Leslie Peck Art Studio on open studio events and seasonal pop-up markets.

As I continue to expand my curatorial practice, I am now offering consultation services for artists, collectors, and organizations. My areas of focus include assistance with private art collections, studio organization, gallery layout and exhibition planning, and curatorial development. Installation services are available on a case-by-case basis.
